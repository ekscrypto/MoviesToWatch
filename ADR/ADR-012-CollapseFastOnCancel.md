## Cancellation is terminal; activities collapse fast and avoid eager state

Decision: Once an activity's lifecycle status enters a cancelling shape (e.g. `IntentClearSearch` while a search debounce is pending), the activity MUST treat that as a terminal signal and unwind to its terminal shape as fast as the in-flight work allows — no further intermediate status updates, no new ephemeral writes. The lifecycle Update Intents an activity emits while running MUST refuse to overwrite a terminal shape with a running-shaped status; the guard lives on the `Intent`, not on the activity's hope that it observed the cancel in time. Activities SHOULD also keep their ephemeral footprint small: every intermediate result an activity mirrors into `PersistentState` or `EphemeralState` is one more late-write surface the cancel path has to defend against. Throttled per-activity progress counters and coarse step transitions are fine while the activity is still running-shaped; bespoke "stash the in-progress result" caches with no other consumer are not.

Reasoning: ADR-001 says the StateMachine processes `Intent`s serially against a single state value, but it does not say activities stop emitting `Intent`s the instant a cancel is dispatched. There is always a window — bounded by the longest in-flight async call — where the activity hasn't yet observed cooperative-cancellation and is about to enqueue another status-update `Intent`. If the `Intent`'s guard accepts every running-shaped status without first checking for the cancelling shape, the late write silently un-cancels the operation: the UI flips back to "running", the user's cancel looks ignored, and the activity continues to its natural terminal. The `Intent`-side guard catches the late write at the StateMachine; the activity-side discipline keeps the late-write surface small in the first place. An activity that emits one throttled progress counter and one terminal `Intent` has a single window to defend; an activity that mirrors every intermediate result into state has many.

## Review Scope

**Strengthens** findings about (a) lifecycle Update `Intent`s whose `mutate(...)` guard does not list the cancelling shape (or the feature's other terminal shapes) among the states it refuses to overwrite with a running-shaped status, (b) activities that continue to enqueue status-update `Intent`s after observing cooperative-cancellation, or (c) activities that mirror intermediate output into `PersistentState` or `EphemeralState` without a consumer that reads it.

**Drops** findings that ask for serialising activities or for rejecting throttled progress Updates while the activity is still in a running shape; the invariant is on terminal-overwrite, not on parallelism.
